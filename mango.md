# Mangoes

## Introduction

Mangoes are tropical stone fruits renowned for their sweet, aromatic flavor and smooth, creamy texture. Originating from South Asia over 4,000 years ago, mangoes are now cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. Often called the "king of fruits," mangoes belong to the Anacardiaceae family and are one of the most consumed fruits globally.

## Nutritional Benefits

Mangoes are nutrient-dense fruits that offer numerous health benefits:

- **Vitamin C**: Rich source of vitamin C, supporting immune function and skin health
- **Vitamin A**: High in beta-carotene, which converts to vitamin A for eye health and immune support
- **Fiber**: Contains dietary fiber that aids digestion and promotes gut health
- **Antioxidants**: Packed with polyphenols and carotenoids that protect against oxidative stress
- **Folate**: Important for cell division and DNA synthesis, especially beneficial during pregnancy
- **Vitamin E**: Supports skin health and provides antioxidant protection
- **Potassium**: Helps regulate blood pressure and maintains heart health
- **Low in Calories**: One cup of mango contains approximately 100 calories

## Common Mango Varieties

### Alphonso
Considered the finest variety, originating from India with a rich, creamy texture and intense sweetness.

### Tommy Atkins
The most widely available commercial variety, known for its disease resistance and long shelf life.

### Kent
Large, juicy mangoes with minimal fiber and a sweet, rich flavor profile.

### Ataulfo (Honey Mango)
Small, golden-yellow mangoes with a creamy, non-fibrous texture and exceptionally sweet taste.

### Haden
One of the parent varieties of many modern cultivars, featuring a sweet-tart flavor with firm flesh.

### Keitt
Large, green-skinned mangoes that remain green even when ripe, with sweet, fiber-free flesh.

## Major Growing Regions

Mangoes require tropical or subtropical climates with distinct wet and dry seasons:

- **India**: The world's largest producer, growing over 40% of global mango production
- **China**: Second largest producer with increasing commercial cultivation
- **Thailand**: Major exporter known for high-quality varieties
- **Mexico**: Leading supplier to North American markets
- **Pakistan**: Renowned for premium varieties like Sindhri and Chaunsa
- **Philippines**: Significant producer of the famous Carabao variety
- **Brazil**: Major producer in South America
- **Egypt**: Important producer in North Africa

## Interesting Facts

- Mango trees can live and bear fruit for over 300 years
- The mango is the national fruit of India, Pakistan, and the Philippines
- Mango leaves are considered sacred in Hindu ceremonies and are used in decorations
- A single mango tree can produce up to 1,000 fruits per year when mature
- Mangoes are related to cashews and pistachios, belonging to the same plant family
- The mango seed can weigh up to 20% of the entire fruit weight
- Unripe mangoes are used to make chutneys, pickles, and tangy beverages in many cultures
- The word "mango" comes from the Tamil word "mangkay" or "mankay"
- Mango wood is used for making furniture and musical instruments like ukuleles
- There are over 1,000 different varieties of mangoes cultivated worldwide
