# Mangoes

## Introduction

Mangoes are tropical stone fruits known as the "king of fruits" and are among the most cultivated fruits in the world. These succulent, aromatic fruits belong to the flowering plant family Anacardiaceae and are native to South Asia, particularly India and Myanmar. With their rich, sweet flavor and smooth texture, mangoes have been cultivated for over 4,000 years and are now grown in tropical and subtropical regions across the globe.

## Nutritional Benefits

Mangoes offer an impressive array of nutrients that support overall health and wellness:

- **Vitamin C**: Excellent source providing immune system support and antioxidant protection
- **Vitamin A**: High in beta-carotene, supporting eye health and vision
- **Folate**: Important for DNA synthesis and cell division, especially beneficial during pregnancy
- **Fiber**: Contains dietary fiber that aids digestion and promotes gut health
- **Vitamin E**: Provides antioxidant protection for cells
- **Vitamin K**: Supports bone health and blood clotting
- **Potassium**: Helps regulate blood pressure and supports heart health
- **Antioxidants**: Rich in polyphenols including mangiferin, which has anti-inflammatory properties
- **Low in Calories**: One cup of mango contains approximately 100 calories

## Popular Mango Varieties

### Alphonso
Considered the finest mango variety, known for its rich, creamy texture and sweet, aromatic flavor. Primarily grown in India.

### Ataulfo (Honey Mango)
Small, golden-yellow mangoes with a smooth, non-fibrous flesh and exceptionally sweet taste.

### Tommy Atkins
The most commonly exported variety, known for its disease resistance, firm flesh, and long shelf life.

### Kent
Large mangoes with a sweet, rich flavor and minimal fiber content, popular in the United States.

### Keitt
Large, green-skinned variety that remains green even when ripe, with a sweet and fruity flavor.

### Haden
One of the oldest commercial varieties, featuring a rich, sweet-tart flavor with aromatic notes.

## Major Growing Regions

Mangoes flourish in warm climates with distinct wet and dry seasons. Key producing regions include:

- **India**: The world's largest producer, accounting for nearly 50% of global mango production
- **China**: Second largest producer with significant domestic consumption
- **Thailand**: Major producer and exporter of tropical mango varieties
- **Indonesia**: Important producer in Southeast Asia
- **Pakistan**: Known for premium varieties like Sindhri and Chaunsa
- **Mexico**: Leading supplier to North American markets
- **Brazil**: Significant producer for both domestic and export markets
- **Egypt**: Major producer in Africa and the Middle East
- **Philippines**: Important producer of Carabao mangoes
- **Bangladesh**: Significant producer with many local varieties

## Interesting Facts

- Mango trees can live and produce fruit for over 300 years
- The mango tree is considered sacred in India and its leaves are used in religious ceremonies
- Mangoes are related to cashews and pistachios, all belonging to the Anacardiaceae family
- The paisley pattern is believed to be inspired by the shape of a mango
- Buddha himself is said to have meditated in a mango grove
- Mango wood is used to make furniture and ukuleles due to its attractive grain
- The mango seed can weigh up to 20% of the entire fruit's weight
- Mangoes are the national fruit of India, Pakistan, and the Philippines
- Over 1,000 different varieties of mangoes exist worldwide
- The largest mango ever recorded weighed 3.435 kg (7.57 pounds)
- Mango leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments
- A mango tree can grow up to 100 feet tall in the wild
