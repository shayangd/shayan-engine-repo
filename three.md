# Three

## Overview

Three is the natural number following two and preceding four. It is the smallest odd prime number and holds significant mathematical, cultural, and symbolic importance across various disciplines.

## Mathematical Properties

- **Prime Number**: Three is the second prime number (after 2)
- **Triangular Number**: The second triangular number (1 + 2 = 3)
- **Fibonacci Number**: Appears in the Fibonacci sequence (1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8...)
- **Factorial**: 3! = 6

## Cultural Significance

The number three appears frequently in culture, religion, and storytelling:
- **Rule of Three**: A writing principle that suggests things that come in threes are more satisfying and effective
- **Three Wishes**: A common theme in folklore and fairy tales
- **Holy Trinity**: In Christianity, the concept of God as three persons
- **Three Primary Colors**: Red, blue, and yellow in traditional color theory

## In Nature

- **Triangles**: The simplest polygon with three sides
- **Three States of Matter**: Solid, liquid, and gas (classical)
- **Three Dimensions**: Length, width, and height in spatial geometry

## Applications

Three is fundamental in various fields:
- **Computer Science**: Ternary logic systems
- **Design**: The rule of thirds in photography and visual composition
- **Sports**: Three points for a field goal in basketball, three strikes in baseball
- **Time**: Three seconds as a common unit for quick reactions
